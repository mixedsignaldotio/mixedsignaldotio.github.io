SRAM Architecture and Memory Cell


Introduction

The static qualifier differentiates SRAM from dynamic random-access memory (DRAM):
1. SRAM will hold its data permanently in the presence of power, while data in DRAM decays in seconds and thus must be periodically refreshed.
2. SRAM is faster than DRAM but it is more expensive in terms of silicon area and cost.
